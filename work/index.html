Final Project: Synth - Mobile Playlist Management

UCSD Cognitive Science 187a: Cognitive Aspects of Digital Design

OVERVIEW

During my senior year at UCSD my group was tasked to create a high fidelity, high resolution wireframe using rapid prototyping tools (balsamiq, invisionapp, axure) along with photoshop to redesign the information architecture for a mobile platform that supports music playing. The system we created supports the construction of playlists organized for different activities. Our process began by interviewing live subjects who listened to music on their mobile phone to identify the most common critical paths that users may take in the creation/management of music playlists.

By interviewing users we were able to generate various competitive analyses with existing mobile music playing solutions to easily identify the most common conventions used. This robust analysis allowed us to determine what kind of information and navigational elements are most important to our target demographic users. This also involved re-naming of common labels throughout each application in order to apply the most informative and concise labels for our own solution.

THE PROCESS

I've been a project manager in the past during one of my internships so naturally I took lead of my group and drafted out a carefully laid plan to determine how we were to layout our application to start designing it. I was able to delegate and complete tasks and set/make deadlines given our incredibly busy schedule during finals week to complete our project to spec and on time.

The idea was to interview different users to get a feel of what kind of situation prompts them to either create or manage a playlist on their phone. We interviewed enough people to generate 27 walkthroughs that detailed distinct scenarios which we used to create a competitive analysis.

We learned that our competitive analysis gave us a baseline goal to aim for when we started to design our app using storyboards and diagrams. We identified a few potential solutions to the most common playlist creation gripe that the majority of users had: having too many stepts to create/manage a playlist:

  1. Remove the options button in favor of a long press
  2. Use a slide right/left menu to open options in favor of a click
  3. Incorporate a drag and drop system to create/manage playlists

After a lengthy discussion about which option would be the best, we figured the best solution would be to "imply" a user clicking by implementing a drag and drop system that avoids any unnecessary actions on the users' part.

THE DESIGN

Once we identified possible solutions, we went to work on creating the high resolution wireframe using photoshop and invisionapp. This is where our major decisions came into play such as how to organize the home page and what options to have for the users in order to make their goal of creating/managing playlists a much more straightforward and fun experience rather than tedious.

One of the more difficult decisions we had was what to include in our menu page. We consulted applications like Spotify (which has a very condensed menu) and Saavn (which has an extensive but powerful menu). We decided to create a menu that had options for editing playlists and browsing through content without feeling too cluttered or lacking.

Our hardest design decision was definitely how to improve the process of adding/removing songs from playlists. We figured that all the apps had the same boring playlist management features in common and this was our opportunity to flex our creativity and make our app stand out. The drag and drop feature we created eliminates the need to have the user perform extra clicks and gives our app a more "added to cart" feel as if they were shopping. This solved both of our goals of making the playlist management process more streamlined, while making it more fun to do.
